<p class="c13"><span class="c1">Denise Mitchell, currently District 4’s council member, is running for her seventh nonconsecutive term. She served as mayor pro tempore from 2013 to 2015.</span></p>
<p class="c13 c14"><span class="c1"></span></p>
<p class="c13"><span class="c1">Mitchell has called College Park home since 1975. Working in the field of early childhood education for over 30 years, according to the city website. The council member aims to improve College Park’s schools for local families. Now retired, Mitchell runs her own educational consultant firm.</span></p>
<p class="c13 c14"><span class="c1"></span></p>
<p class="c13"><span class="c1">Mitchell says her other goals as a council member include prioritizing affordable housing for students and for adults over the age of 55 living in College Park, who are hoping to downsize in their retirement, Mitchell said.</span></p>
<p class="c13 c14"><span class="c1"></span></p>
<p class="c13"><span class="c1">She also plans to continue discussions regarding the city’s infrastructure to make College Park more inviting, walkable and safe, as well as to reduce traffic in College Park’s major intersections.</span></p>
<p class="c13 c14"><span class="c1"></span></p>
<p class="c13"><span class="c1">Mitchell is passionate about enhancing the “vibrancy” of College Park and learning from residents and students about their perspectives on what needs to change in the city.</span></p> 
<p class="c13 c14"><span class="c1"></span></p>
<p class="c13"><span class="c1">“I am very much approachable, very much seeking their input,” Mitchell said. “[I] will always represent them.”</span></p> 
<p class="c13 c14"><span class="c1"></span></p>
<p class="c13"><span class="c1">Mitchell says one of her greatest values as a council member is speaking with constituents “from the heart.” Simply striking a balance between those on either side and being inclusive can solve local issues, according to the District 4 council member.</span></p> 
<p class="c13 c14"><span class="c1"></span></p>
<p class="c13"><span class="c1">“It's not always about winning,” Mitchell said. “So I firmly believe that you have to build a relationship and be collaborative, be respectful, and that takes you a long way.”</span></p>
